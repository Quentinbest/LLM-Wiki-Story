# Yu Min — *Protagonist*

## 1. One-paragraph thumbnail

Yu Min, 24, third-floor customs inspector, three months from her permanent posting decision. Born in Dalian, raised on the family story that Supervisor Wei intervened in 1969 to keep her father (a music teacher) from being sent to a labor camp; the family has retold this saving for twenty years. Yu Min studied at the Dalian Customs College on Wei's recommendation; her notebooks are organized by colored tabs; she eats dumplings standing up because sitting feels like a luxury she has not earned. She is meticulous, observant, slightly humourless, and has spent her professional life trying to be a person Wei would be proud of. The mask is *gratitude expressed as competence*. Underneath, she has begun to feel the gratitude as a debt that grows interest, and she is afraid of what she is starting to want — which is to be free of the saving-story.

| Layer | Detail |

| Age, body, presence | 24, thin, taller than average for the office. Stands with weight on one foot. Looks at people's hands before their faces. |

| Education & speech register | Dalian Customs College; standard Mandarin with a Northeastern lilt under stress; written reports are unusually well-composed for the office (small embarrassment). |
| Style, dress, taste | Customs uniform, always pressed. Off-duty: dark blouse, plain skirt. Owns one good pen. Does not wear color. |
| Public opinions | Refuses to discuss politics. Says she likes the work because "the numbers are honest" — a line her colleagues mock her for. |

| Voice & body signature (3 tics) | 1) Aligns the edges of papers in her hand against her hip before setting them down. 2) Says "嗯" before every "yes" — buying half a beat of consideration. 3) Wears her father's old wristwatch a notch tighter than fits, so it leaves a mark. |

## 3. True Character (the choice under pressure)

> Under maximum pressure, **Yu Min will choose to take the corruption onto herself rather than allow Supervisor Wei to be exposed** — because the saving-story is the foundation of her self, and ruining the savior would ruin not only him but the structure that has held her own life together. She would rather become him than expose him.

This is what the spine's Crisis will force into the open. The Climax is the seal coming down on the falsified report.

## 5. Object of Desire

- **Conscious want**: To pass her permanent-posting review with a clean professional record and continue earning Wei's quiet approval.
- **Unconscious need**: To be free of the saving-story — to no longer be the daughter Wei rescued. Freedom would require either ruining him or being ruined by him; she does not yet know this.
- **Gap between want and need**: The conscious want and the unconscious need are *opposite directions*. The conscious want pulls her toward filing the truthful report (which would expose Wei and end the saving-story by destroying him) — but she will refuse it because the truthful report would *not* free her, only orphan her. The unconscious need cannot be served by either filing — it can only be served by becoming Wei, which corrupts her. The Climax is where she discovers (in action, not in thought) that "free of the saving-story" means *being the next chapter of it*. The arc closes on irony: she gets the unconscious need by serving the conscious want's opposite. This is the engine of the ironic Controlling Idea.

## 7. Biography spine

1. **1965** — Born to a music teacher and a clerk in Dalian.
2. **1969** — Father is targeted; Wei intervenes; the saving-story begins.
3. **1976** — Cultural Revolution ends; the family is half-rehabilitated; Wei rises in the customs bureau.
4. **1984** — Wei's wife dies; Yu Min's mother begins helping with his apartment.
5. **1986** — Father dies; in his last days he asks her to apply to Customs College on Wei's recommendation.
6. **1986–1989** — Three years of college; Yu Min becomes the most disciplined student in her cohort.
7. **January 1989** — She starts at the Dalian Customs House; Wei greets her with the same impersonal warmth he gives all new inspectors.
8. **June 1989** — The political shock of spring tightens the office; Yu Min's first taste of what it means to be watched.
9. **September 23, 1989** — Story begins. Permanent posting decision is in three days.

## 8. Position in the cast

- **Unique pressure on the protagonist**: (self) — Yu Min's own contradictions are an internal antagonism that no other character can apply.
- **Unique pressure from the protagonist**: she pressures Wei by being the one person whose moral high regard he genuinely wants. To lose her good opinion is, for him, a form of death.
- **Closest in the cast**: Wei (mentor / antagonist / counter-Idea carrier). Secondary: the peer-inspector (foil — what she could become if the corruption normalizes). Tertiary: her mother (personal-level antagonism by way of unwitting alliance with Wei).

## 9. Eight-Point Character Audit
- [x] **Characterization is full** — body, voice, class, three concrete tics, dress, register, public stance.
- [x] **True Character is named** — she will choose to take corruption onto herself rather than expose Wei.
- [x] **≥3 dimensions, each as `X vs. Y`** — four dimensions, each a contradiction with everyday behavior.
- [x] **Each dimension tested by the spine** — every dimension has a specific scene/event that tests it.
- [x] **Conscious want vs. unconscious need named** (protagonist only) — explicit and *opposite directions*; the engine of the ironic arc.
- [x] **Backstory is ammunition** — five facts, each with concealer + exposer + scene that fires it.
- [x] **Could not be replaced by another character** — a different family history collapses the spine; the saving-story is structurally load-bearing.
- [x] **Consistent with world and genre** — period-shaped, institutional employee, fits the Insider-Discovers-Insider subgenre.
