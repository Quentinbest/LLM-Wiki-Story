# 声音锚点 ——《废徒倒丹》

Seven voice dimensions extracted by close reading of passages across all four acts. Treat as binding constraints for Pass 5 (voice drift repair) and all future prose drafting.

---

## Dimension 1 — Vocabulary Range

**Rule**: Minimal lexicon. Concrete, physical-action nouns and verbs only.

Permitted registers:
- Classical cultivation-world proper nouns: 丹道、乾元、苍生之炉、戒尺、印记、银砂、倒挂、丹田
- Vernacular Chinese short-form action verbs: 举、落、按、抖、走、转、叫、看、闭
- Physical state adjectives only when measurable: 断、旧、金黑色、暗赤、清明
- Numbers and counts as prose, not as data: 第一步、三年来、七日七夜

Forbidden:
- Literary flourish vocabulary: 凄然、怅惘、肃穆、苍凉
- Psychological abstract nouns used as narrator statements: 孤独、救赎、意义、代价
- Modern register intrusions: 其实、毕竟、因为所以、以至于

**Anchor line**: 主角举断戒尺。（不是"主角沉重地举起了那把断裂的戒尺"）

---

## Dimension 2 — Sentence Architecture

**Rule**: Declarative fragments. Parallel negation. No compound sentences.

Patterns that define the voice:
- Single-action sentence: `主角转身。` / `戒尺仍举着未落。`
- Triple negation parallel: `不是悲。不是喜。不是恨。`
- `不是X。是Y。` denial-then-replacement formula
- Counting cadence: `第一步。第二步。第三步。`
- Named-then-described: `晨雾。` (paragraph break) `从地面生起。` (next paragraph)

Forbidden:
- Subordinate clauses with 虽然/尽管/即使 governing the main clause
- 于是/因此/所以 connecting sentences
- Appositive noun phrases: "主角，那个三年来……的人，举起了戒尺"

**Anchor line**: 不是悲。不是喜。不是恨。不是愧。不是释怀。不是不释怀。是**面无可读情绪**——

---

## Dimension 3 — Rhythm

**Rule**: Staccato. Every beat is its own paragraph. `——` carries the white space.

`——` usage rules:
- One `——` = single breath pause (standard transition between actions)
- Three consecutive `——` = heavier pause (before or after a turning point)
- Five or more `——` in a row = silence accumulation before a major revelation
- Never two consecutive content-lines without at least one `——` between them

Line economy:
- Maximum one action per content paragraph
- Maximum one sentence per line that carries emotional charge
- Long distance between two lines makes them louder, not quieter

**Anchor sequence** (from scene-4-4-5):
```
主角——

——

——

——

——

——

——

**转身**。
```

---

## Dimension 4 — Register / Distance

**Rule**: Camera-eye. Narrator observes physical facts only. Never names interiority except via the body.

Permitted interiority channels:
- Bodily: 胸口烫疤跳了一下 / 右手腕不抖 / 左腿失重半寸
- Negative space: 他没有看师妹。他没有看炉。（什么都没看的枚举比描写了"什么"更重）
- Denial of expected emotion: 不是身体疲惫的抖。
- Action-as-interior: 他没有捡令牌。（one physical non-action carries the moral weight）

Forbidden:
- Narrator-stated emotion: "他心里有些酸"/ "他感到一阵空旷"
- Free indirect discourse that names psychological state: "他知道这是他最后一次了"
- Authorial sympathy intrusions: "他多年来从未……"

**Anchor line**: 他**没有捡令牌**。（令牌仍在主角左脚旁约一寸。）

---

## Dimension 5 — Tense / POV

**Rule**: Present tense action. Tight third person. Narrator has camera access, not mind access.

Tense rules:
- All action: present tense 举/走/转/落
- Backstory when needed: past tense only within clearly framed flashback clause, never in narrator voice
- Duration statements permitted: 三年来 / 七日七夜 / Day 5 到 Day 7

POV rules:
- Narrator knows: what the character does, what the character says, physical state of character's body
- Narrator does not know: what the character is thinking unless it surfaces in speech or action
- Exception: when narrator reports a decision, it is stated as action, not thought: `主角做出决定。` + blank lines + `**起炉自死**。`

**Anchor**: `主角在举到最高位的姿势中——做出决定——起炉自死。`（决定即动作，非内省）

---

## Dimension 6 — Taboo Words

**Rule**: Specific words that break voice on contact. Never use these in narrator voice.

Hard taboo (never):
- 感到、觉得、意识到、明白了（emotion-reporting verbs）
- 显然、当然、毕竟、确实（narrator editorial）
- 救赎、承担、选择了（abstract moral register — narrator cannot say these）
- 终于、竟然、居然（surprise-editorializing）

Soft taboo (only via character action/speech, never narrator):
- 痛 — can appear in physical context (按炉壁两息 = 烫穿), never as abstract
- 爱 — never appears in this story in any voice
- 对不起 — never appears; absence is part of the story's structure

**Verification test**: If the word could appear in a therapist's session notes or a literary review, it cannot appear in narrator voice.

---

## Dimension 7 — Anti-Patterns

**Rule**: Specific constructions that signal voice drift. Treat as hard flags during Pass 5.

Anti-pattern list:

| Anti-pattern | What it signals | Typical fix |
|---|---|---|
| `他感到/他觉得 + [emotion noun]` | Narrator entering mind | Cut to body: 烫疤跳 / 手抖 |
| `[action]，因为[reason]。` | Narrator explaining motivation | Delete reason; action carries it |
| Trailing parenthetical after action | Annotation residue | Delete parenthetical |
| `这是[abstract label]的[noun]。` | Design language in prose | Delete or replace with action |
| `第N次入画` / `累积重量` | Image system tracking language | Delete; motif should be felt, not counted |
| `按[[X]]…` wikilink | Scaffolding residue | Delete |
| `按宗门律…` followed by bureaucratic explanation | World-rule over-explainer | Trim to minimum or cut |
| Emotional adjective before body action | Editorializing | Cut adjective; keep only action |

---

## Voice-Drift Boundary Test

A passage is in-voice if:

1. You can remove all paragraphs except the `——` separators and the action lines, and the story still reads.
2. No paragraph names what the reader should feel.
3. The longest sentence has fewer than 20 Chinese characters.
4. The word "感到" or "觉得" does not appear in narrator voice.
5. Every "不是X。是Y。" denial names only physical facts on both sides.

A passage has drifted if:

1. A full explanatory clause appears that could be cut without losing any story information.
2. The narrator describes the same emotional fact twice (once in body language, once in vocabulary).
3. Any sentence could be spoken by the narrator of a literary novel set in modern China.
