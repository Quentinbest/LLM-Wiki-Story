# Setting Survey — The Third Day

## 1. The four dimensions

| Dimension | Position | Why this and not wider |
|---|---|---|
| Period | September 23–25, 1989 | Three months after the spring; the post-event surveillance tightening is real and dated. Earlier loses the political weight; later (1990s reform) destroys the institution that makes the trap work. |
| Duration | Three days + a six-month Resolution coda | The clock is the antagonism. Any longer and the trap loosens; any shorter and the moral architecture collapses into thriller pacing. The coda is essential for the replication image. |
| Location | The Dalian Customs House, third floor, with the bonded warehouse district one street over | A single building gives us the institutional grammar; the warehouses give us the *physical* crime. The apartment block is the personal-level register. The dumpling restaurant is the only neutral ground. |
| Root level of conflict | **Inner** | The Idea is about gratitude curdling. Outer events serve the inner trap. Personal-level antagonism (Wei) and extra-personal antagonism (the bureau, the broker, the political climate) are *channels* for the inner crisis. |

## 2. World — one paragraph

The Dalian Customs House in 1989 is a working office, not a fortress: walls of pigeonhole shelves stuffed with manifests, three rotary phones per floor, a single typewriter that takes priority over the inspectors' clipboards. The third floor smells of coal dust from the harbor and stale tea. Everyone watches everyone — not paranoiacally, but as a baseline social reflex; the political year has tuned the office to a dull, alert hum. Bonded shipments arrive on a precise tide-driven schedule and must be cleared before the foreman's whistle at 18:00 or be held until morning. The supervisor's office on the fourth floor has a window onto the harbor; the inspectors' shared room on the third floor faces the rail yard. The dumpling restaurant outside the customs gate is the only place where the institution's people speak as people.

## 3. Rules of engagement

1. **Rule:** All bonded-warehouse manifests require *two* seals: the on-duty inspector's and the supervisor's countersign. *(Source: customs procedure.)* — *Forbids* a clean "Yu Min files alone." The Climax must contain Wei's seal alongside hers, or her false signature.
2. **Rule:** A discrepancy between manifest and physical inventory must be reported to the bureau within 24 hours, in writing, by the discovering inspector. *(Source: customs procedure.)* — *Forbids* sitting on the discovery indefinitely. The 24-hour clock cuts inside the three-day clock.
3. **Rule:** Permanent posting requires the supervisor's written endorsement. *(Source: institutional regulation.)* — *Forbids* Yu Min escaping by being transferred without Wei's knowledge.
4. **Rule:** Foreigners and overseas Chinese arriving by ship after 18:00 must be processed at the morning shift; their luggage is held overnight in 4-East. *(Source: customs procedure.)* — *Permits* a specific kind of smuggling: a delayed arrival → unattended luggage → manifest swap before morning.
5. **Rule:** Letters and packages between mainland customs employees and Hong Kong are routinely opened. *(Source: 1989 political climate.)* — *Forbids* Yu Min using mail to a Hong Kong contact.
6. **Rule:** Office phone calls after 17:30 require the duty officer's logbook entry. *(Source: institutional regulation.)* — *Forbids* clandestine calls without a paper trail.
7. **Rule:** The foreman's whistle at 18:00 marks shift end; cargo movement is forbidden between 18:00 and 06:00 except by written exemption from the supervisor. *(Source: port procedure.)* — *Permits* a specific kind of crime: written exemptions are forgeable, and Wei has the authority to issue them.
8. **Rule:** Within the customs employee housing blocks, every neighbor is in some sense a coworker. *(Source: 工人新村 social fact.)* — *Forbids* private grief at home; restricts where Yu Min can think.

## 4. What this setting forbids (story moves)

1. Cannot resolve by phoning a journalist — the after-hours call is logged.
2. Cannot escape via mail to a Hong Kong outsider — letters are opened.
3. Cannot have a clean unilateral filing — two seals required.
4. Cannot privately grieve at home — the apartment block is too thin-walled.
5. Cannot extend past 18:00 day three — the whistle ends the clock.

## 5. What this setting demands

1. **The whistle**: 18:00 must mark at least three load-bearing beats — at least one act break and the Climax.
2. **The two-seal rule**: the Climax must include the doubled signature, real or false. Image: two stamps, one pad.
3. **The dumpling restaurant**: the only space where Yu Min and Wei can speak as people; obligatory location for the Private Confrontation (Obligatory Scene 2).
4. **The bonded warehouse 4-East after dark**: the physical crime must occur there; we need at least one scene set in 4-East after 18:00 to make the racket *visible* rather than rhetorical.
5. **The political year**: at least one beat where the surveillance tightens around her specifically (a phone call observed, a letter delayed).

## 6. Authenticity vs. accuracy

| Element | Accuracy | Authenticity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customs procedure for bonded shipments, 1989 | high | high | The genre's core convention; audience trusts (or breaks) on this |
| Layout of Dalian Customs House | low | high | Audience cannot fact-check the building; needs to *feel* like a 1980s state institution |
| The 18:00 whistle and shift schedule | medium | high | Period port practice, broadly correct in spirit |
| Dialect / colloquial register | low | high | Northeastern Chinese cadence, not phrasebook Mandarin |
| Specific names of customs forms | low | medium | Plausible-sounding > technically correct |
| 工人新村 housing block details | medium | high | A reader who lived in one will check |
| Ship traffic from Hong Kong, 1989 | high | medium | Historically real; check date-specific shipping records for one named vessel |
| Tide tables for late September 1989 | high | low | Used once at Climax; must be real |

## 7. Research targets — triaged

| Priority | Target | Method | What changes if the answer surprises us |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P0** | Customs procedure for bonded warehouses, post-1985 reform, pre-WTO | Read 1980s customs manuals (digitized 海关总署 archives, 1985–1990); consult retired inspector if reachable | If the two-seal rule did not apply this way, the Climax must restage; the trap reshapes. |
| **P0** | Tide and harbor schedule for Dalian, late September 1989 | Historical shipping records | If the 18:00 whistle is fictional for this port, choose a different real shift marker. |
| **P1** | 1989 surveillance climate, customs sector specifically | Memoirs of late-80s customs employees; secondary literature on 1989 institutional aftermath | Affects scene texture (rule 5, rule 6); does not move the spine. |
| **P1** | Hong Kong–Dalian shipping route, 1989 | One named vessel and route | Adds a specific ship name to ground the manifest discrepancy. |
| **P2** | Dialect register for 1980s Dalian customs employees | Native consultation | Dialogue authenticity; can be tuned in revision. |
| **P2** | Period-specific objects: typewriter model, manifest forms, seal pad | Visual reference (museum photographs) | Set-dressing only. |

P0 items must resolve before scene work commits.

## 9. Open questions for the writer
- Is the writer comfortable committing to historical 1989, or does the project want a fictional analogous regime to widen reach? (1989 is sharper; fictional regime is safer for international markets.)
- Should the apartment block be Yu Min's only home, or do we add a second domestic location (e.g. her parents' separate apartment)? (Single is tighter; second adds personal-level antagonism surface.)
- Do we want the Hong Kong end of the smuggling route to become a location, or remain off-stage as a name? (Off-stage preserves the institutional-claustrophobia tone.)
