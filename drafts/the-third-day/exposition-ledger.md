## 2. Talking-head violations

(Audited from existing Scene 14 + planned scenes.)

Currently: **none confirmed**. The only borderline candidate is Scene 14's beat 4 (the father's letter content), which is dialogue-driven exposition. **However**, it passes the talking-head test: Wei is *not* informing Yu Min of facts she already knows; he is informing her of a fact (the existence and content of the letter) she did *not* know. The delivery is also *combat* — he is using the letter to position her. Per the audit rule, this is direct delivery at a high-stakes moment (the Private Confrontation, which is an obligatory scene), which is permitted.

If `subtext-whisperer` were to recommend cutting beat 4, the audit would lose its single dialogue-driven exposition delivery — and the saving-story would have to be planted entirely in S1.3 + PC2. That is feasible but loses the *combat* delivery in PC3. Recommendation: **keep beat 4**.

## 5. Smuggle proposals (per high-priority fact)

### Fact #3 — The saving-story (general → specific via the father's letter)

The story has *two* releases of the saving-story; they must not duplicate. Each carries a different version:

- **S1.3 release (general, unreliable):** Yu Min tells Lin Xue at the dumpling stall after the discovery. The audience hears the family-canonical version: *Wei intervened in 1969; her father stayed; Wei rose in the bureau; the family is grateful; that is why she is here.* This version is *Yu Min's narration of it*. Lin Xue does not contradict; she nods and changes the subject — which is the audience's first sign that the version is incomplete.
- **Scene 14 / PC3 release (specific, weaponized):** Wei delivers the father's-letter content directly. This version is *Wei's narration of it*, and it adds the line "I will not thank you, because thanks would put me in your debt." The audience now has *two* versions of the same backstory and reads the gap between them: the family's version (gratitude) vs. the father's actual position (refusal of debt). The Idea's irony is set up by this gap — Yu Min has spent her life enacting *gratitude* on a father who chose *refusal*.

This is McKee's distributed-not-dumped principle at work. The two versions also pass the rule: each release *escalates the present scene*. S1.3's release escalates Yu Min's resistance to filing (she becomes more cautious); Scene 14's release escalates the dilemma to its irreducible form.

**No further smuggling needed.** Verdict on fact 3: **smuggle confirmed across two scenes**.

### Fact #9 — Captain Zhang as embodied racket

The genre contract demands the racket be *embodied* (one named broker), not abstract. But the institutional-claustrophobia tone forbids his on-stage appearance. The smuggle:

- **Inciting Incident (S1.2)**: Yu Min reads the signature aloud to herself while cross-checking. The name "张" is uttered by the protagonist's mouth in private — combat delivery: she is fighting to identify what she is seeing.
- **PC4 / S2.4 morning**: Lin Xue is on the phone in the office when Yu Min arrives. Yu Min overhears one sentence: *"船长 已经 准备好了"* (the captain is ready). The voice on the other end is heard for one beat — gravelly, male, the dialect not fully Northeast. Lin Xue ends the call without acknowledging it. Yu Min's eyes meet Lin Xue's; they do not speak about it.

This is **combat delivery** (Yu Min fighting to identify the voice-context) + **action implication** (Lin Xue's refusal to acknowledge what Yu Min has just heard) layered. The racket is now embodied without ever appearing.

**Verdict on fact 9**: **smuggle confirmed in two scenes via combined techniques**.

- [x] **Every load-bearing fact has a fighter** — all 12 facts have at least one fighter; facts 1, 2, 7, 8, 10, 11 are world-rules or atmospherics that don't require interpersonal fighters and are delivered via set-dressing/action-implication.
- [x] **No "as you know, Bob"** — confirmed; the only dialogue-driven exposition (Scene 14 beat 4) is information *new* to Yu Min, delivered under combat.
- [x] **Backstory withheld until needed** — fact 3 (saving-story) released in stages across Acts 1–2; fact 4 (father's last wish) released only when the dumplings-at-the-gate triangle is present to receive it; no backstory front-loaded into Act 1 dumps.
- [x] **Each backstory release escalates the present scene** — confirmed in §3 release schedule; every release has a named present-tense escalation.
- [x] **Combat delivery preferred over direct** — 6 facts use combat delivery (3, 4, 5, 9, 12, plus the combat-refined version of 3 in Scene 14); 4 use action-implication or set-dressing (1, 2, 6, 8, 10, 11); 1 uses interior-monologue (fact 7, prose only); 0 use direct delivery without combat.
- [x] **Flashbacks are scenes that turn** — n/a; no flashbacks.
- [x] **Voice-over carries only what scenes can't** — n/a; no voice-over.
- [x] **Distributed, not dumped** — no scene delivers more than 1–2 major facts. Heaviest: PC2 / S2.1 carries 1 backstory fact (fact 4) + 1 atmospheric (fact 10); Scene 14 carries 1 backstory fact (fact 3-deep) and 1 inner-state (fact 7's setup priming); both within tolerance.
