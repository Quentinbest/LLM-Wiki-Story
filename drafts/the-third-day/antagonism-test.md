# Antagonism Test — The Third Day

## 1. Negative Spectrum ladder (primary value)

> **Primary value**: integrity / complicity (alt: moral high ground / moral debt)

| Rung | Form | Carrier in this story | Where it shows |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Positive** | *integrity*: doing the work cleanly under the institution's procedures, with one's seal honest | Yu Min as protagonist; her clean-seal reputation; her Day 1 morning routine | story-start (S1.1); briefly held into Act 1's first half |
| **Contradictory** | *complicity*: looking the other way at known wrongdoing | Lin Xue (already chose); Yu Min's two prior look-aways (fact 5) | revealed in S2.2 (Lin Xue raises the prior look-aways); embodied throughout Act 2 |
| **Contrary** | *complicity disguised as procedure*: complicity carried out *through* the institution's official forms (Wei's exemption letters; the office's "not knowing is the rule") | Wei's exemption signatures; Lin Xue's revelation in S1.3 ("the office already knows; not knowing is the rule") | Act 1 close into mid-Act 2; the Private Confrontation makes it most legible |
| **Negation of the Negation** | *complicity that wears the form of integrity, performed out of love for the corrupting figure*: a forged signature placed *carefully*, with professional precision, *for the sake of the savior*; corruption disguised — even from the protagonist — as fidelity | **Yu Min at the Climax** | Day 3 17:58 — the two seals; the drying ink; the whistle |

**Reach**: **Negation of the Negation**.
**Verdict**: the Negative Spectrum reaches all four rungs. The ladder is fully populated. The fourth rung is structurally *required* by the ironic Controlling Idea, and the Climax is where it lands.

## 5. The Crisis dilemma test

> **The Crisis must force a choice between two irreconcilable goods, or the lesser of two evils.**

| Option A | Option B | Are they truly irreconcilable? | What forces the fork? |
|---|---|---|---|
| File the truthful manifest. The racket ends; Wei is exposed; Yu Min's professional integrity is preserved; the saving-story is destroyed; the man who saved her family is ruined. | File the falsified manifest. Wei is preserved; the racket continues; Yu Min becomes the new face of the corruption; her professional integrity is forged-over in a single signature. | **Yes.** Removing either horn destroys the meaning of the other. Without A, B is not a moral choice — just a corrupt drift; without B, A is not the only path — it is the inevitable one and Yu Min has no choice to make. Each horn is *what makes the other a choice*. | The five Progressive Complications close every other escape: PC1 (institutional), PC2 (familial cost), PC3 (Wei's silent endorsement — "I cannot confront him"), PC4 (Lin Xue's "they escape themselves" — "I cannot leave"), PC5 (Officer Hou — "I cannot wait"). All three levels of conflict are simultaneously engaged at Crisis. |

**Test passed.** This is a true dilemma. (Independently confirmed by `crisis-climax-audit.md` §1, run earlier — cross-validation between agents.)

Sub-test: **could the dilemma be solved by a more clever protagonist?** No. Every clever solution has been tried in Acts 1–2 and closed by the Progressive Complications. Smartness does not escape this trap; the trap is moral, not procedural. This is the test that distinguishes a true dilemma from a hard choice.

## 6. Counter-Idea embodiment check

> **Counter-Idea (from `controlling-idea.md`)**: *Loyalty to the one who saved you is the highest moral act, more sacred than the law.*

| Carrier | Where they argue it (in scene) | Could the audience plausibly agree with them in that scene? |
|---|---|---|
| **Wei** | Scene 14 (PC3) — the father's-letter beat (4): he tells Yu Min the story of how her father refused the debt and *did the work*. By offering this as a moral framework rather than as a request, he embodies the Counter-Idea at its strongest. He does not plead. He shows. | **Yes — strongly.** A morally serious viewer hearing the father's-letter line ("I will not thank you, because thanks would put me in your debt, and I refuse the debt; I will simply do my work") feels the position. Wei is not arguing for *silence about the racket*; he is arguing for *a way of being in the world that refuses transactional morality*. The audience cannot dismiss him as a villain; the Counter-Idea is alive in his mouth. |
| **Lin Xue** | PC4 (S2.4) — the "they escape themselves" line. Her carrier-form is *the alternate Counter-Idea*: even escape is corruption, and the only honest path within the system is to remain. | **Yes — bitterly.** The audience reads her line as the *cost* of having made the wrong choice. Her version of the Counter-Idea is the version Wei's would become without his moral seriousness — and that is exactly why she is the foil. |
| **The mother** (carrier of Idea's setup, not its argument) | S2.1 — the dumplings at the gate; the saving-story redoubled to Wei. | n/a — the mother does not *argue* the Counter-Idea; she *enacts* it unironically. The audience reads her behavior as innocent and correct *on its own terms* (a daughter cleaning the apartment of the man who saved her husband is not a moral failure). The mother's correctness is what makes the Idea ironic. |

**Verdict**: the Counter-Idea has at least two persuasive embodiments (Wei's strong version, Lin Xue's bitter version) plus one *unironic carrier* (the mother). All three are characters the audience must take seriously as moral beings. The Counter-Idea is not a straw. **Confirmed.**

(Note: this is the audit point that distinguishes ironic Ideas from preachy ones. If Wei had been written as a villain, the irony would collapse and the story would tilt toward melodrama. The cast-design's discipline of "Wei is morally serious; Lin Xue is morally serious in a different way" is what passes this test.)

- [x] **All three levels engaged across spine** — heatmap §3 shows × at all three levels for PC3, Crisis, Climax. Inner is continuously engaged. No structural-pivot scene has fewer than two ×s; pivot scenes (PC3, Crisis, Climax) have three.
- [x] **Negative Spectrum reaches at least Contrary; Negation of the Negation by Crisis** — ladder §1 reaches all four rungs; rung 4 lands at Climax (slightly past Crisis, which is the moment of *recognition* of rung 4, with the *enactment* at Climax). Both moments engage rung 4.
- [x] **Antagonism escalates monotonically by act** — act-end magnitudes: Act 1 ≈ −2, Act 2 ≈ −3 (with internal False Ending bump), Act 3 / Climax = −2 corrupted-positive (the strongest reversal). Confirmed in `act-design.md` §4 rhythm chart.
- [x] **Antagonist's resources > protagonist's for most of story** — confirmed in §2 force balance table; Yu Min sits at or below from the Inciting Incident through the Climax. The single recovery is the False Ending — structurally correct.
- [x] **Risk is real at every act end** — Act 1 end: will she file by morning? The audience does not know. Act 2 end: will she take the transfer? Brief audience belief that she might. Act 3: will the seal land *truthfully or falsely*? The audience does not know which manifest she has drafted.
- [x] **Crisis is a true dilemma** — §5 test passed. Independently confirmed by `crisis-climax-audit.md`.
- [x] **Inner antagonism dramatized, not announced** — Yu Min's contradictions are visible in her behavior throughout: the upside-down logbook (Landmark 2), the watch tightening, the rehearsal-of-three-openers in Scene 14, the held breath at Crisis. No confidant monologue.
- [x] **Counter-Idea has a persuasive embodiment** — Wei carries it strongly; Lin Xue carries an alternate version; mother carries the setup unironically. §6 test passed.

## 8. Verdict

**GREEN.** The antagonism is sufficient at every spine event, escalates correctly across acts, reaches Negation of the Negation at the Climax, dramatizes inner conflict throughout, and supplies the Counter-Idea with two strong human carriers. The Crisis is a true dilemma. No structural failures.

This is, structurally, the cleanest antagonism profile possible for an ironic Controlling Idea — the contracts have done their job. The spine + cast + setting are *each* contributing antagonism on different axes, and the axes do not overlap (Wei = personal + extra-personal + Counter-Idea-of-loyalty; Lin Xue = personal + foil + Counter-Idea-of-escape; Officer Hou = extra-personal + clock; Captain Zhang = extra-personal-embodiment; the world rules = institutional pressure; Yu Min's own dimensions = continuous inner antagonism). The architecture is holding.

Tunings (none threatening green):

1. **Verify in scene work** that the inner antagonism shows on the page through the body-tics established in `yu-min.md` §2 (paper-edges-against-hip; "嗯" before yes; the watch). The risk in inner-rooted antagonism is that it stays inside the protagonist's head; the body-tic discipline is the cure.
2. **Lin Xue's antagonism** depends on her being *present enough* in Acts 1–2 for her PC4 line to land. Her four scenes (S1.3, S2.2, S2.4 morning, S2.4 close) are sufficient if `scene-architect` gives her body-presence and not just dialogue.
3. **Captain Zhang's restriction to signature + voice** preserves the institutional-claustrophobia tone but means the racket-as-embodied-criminal antagonism is delivered indirectly. The two delivery beats (signature at Inciting Incident; voice at S2.4) must be vivid; otherwise the racket reads as abstract. `scene-architect` to design those moments with care.
