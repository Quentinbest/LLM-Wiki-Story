# Scene 14 — The Endorsement

## 1. Frame
- **Where/when**: Wei's fourth-floor office, Day 2, 16:00. Window onto the harbor; sun low; coal-dust haze. Wei behind a steel desk with a felt blotter; a calendar showing the 25th circled in red (her posting decision date). Two chairs facing the desk; she takes the right one. The radio is on, low — a forecast.
- **Who's in it**: Yu Min, Supervisor Wei (mid-fifties, lean, tired). No one else.
- **Spine position**: PC3 — Mid-Act 2 — Private Confrontation. Genre Contract's **Obligatory Scene 2**.

## 2. Objectives & antagonism
- **POV scene objective** (active verb the actor can play): **to extract from Wei an acknowledgement of the racket — without naming it — so that she can file the truthful report without ruining him.** (She is here looking for permission.)
- **Forces of antagonism in this scene**:
  - *Personal*: Wei himself — but the antagonism is not opposition; it is *refusal to give her the dialogue she came for*. He neither denies nor pleads. The hardest kind of antagonism: a man being morally serious in a way she did not prepare for.
  - *Inner*: her gratitude, audible in her own throat as she tries to speak.
  - *Extra-personal*: the calendar; the endorsement letter on his blotter; the institutional fact that he is about to sign her future.
- **Other characters' secret objectives**:
  - Wei: to give her the endorsement *without* giving her any other thing — not denial, not confession, not absolution. He is letting her decide.

## 3. Value
- **Named value**: **moral high ground / moral debt** (or, in McKee terms, *integrity / complicity*).
- **Opening charge**: **+** (Yu Min enters with the moral high ground; she has the discrepancy in her hand, even if she has not put it on his desk; she is the one with knowledge).
- **Closing charge**: **−** (she leaves with the endorsement in her bag, carrying the moral debt; Wei's silence has placed the choice on her, and the only way to take the choice is to assume the debt). *Charge flips.*

## 4. Beats

Six beats.

1. **Approach / probe-reveal.** Yu Min enters. Wei does not look up immediately; he is reading something — *not* her file. She crosses the linoleum, hand on the back of the chair, before sitting. He says, "嗯, 坐." She sits.
   - **Action**: she enters, her hand aligning the edge of her notebook against her hip (her tic).
   - **Expectation**: he will look at her; she will read his face.
   - **Reaction**: he keeps reading; he is not avoiding her, simply finishing.
   - **Gap**: small.

2. **The opening / probe-reveal.** Wei sets down what he was reading and looks at her. He says, "Your father would be proud, you know. The summary you wrote on the Korean shipment last week. He used to play a piece by Bach that began the way that report read." Yu Min, who has rehearsed three different ways to bring up the discrepancy, finds herself smiling at her own shoes.
   - **Action**: she means to say *Supervisor, I need to ask you about the night exemption from the 22nd*; she says, "Thank you, Supervisor."
   - **Expectation**: he will move to office matters.
   - **Reaction**: he picks up her endorsement letter — already typed, already addressed to the bureau — and he uncaps his pen.
   - **Gap**: medium. She is being moved past the conversation she came for.

3. **The first attempt / push-resist.** She speaks before he can sign.
   - **Action**: "Supervisor — I — there's a manifest from the 22nd. The bonded shipment from Yantian. I wanted to ask you—"
   - **Expectation**: he will interrupt with a denial, an explanation, a flicker, anything. She has prepared for everything except what he does.
   - **Reaction**: he says, "Yes." That is all. He waits.
   - **Gap**: wide. She has been given silence to fill; she has nothing prepared for silence.

4. **The pivot / confessing in fragments — by him, not by her.** Into her silence, he speaks — but not about the manifest. He tells a story she has heard once before from her mother, but never from him: how in 1969 he got her father out of the labor-camp roster by claiming a clerical reassignment that needed a music teacher *here*. He says, "Your father wrote me one letter afterwards. Only one. He wrote, 'I will not thank you, because thanks would put me in your debt, and I refuse the debt; I will simply do my work.' I have kept that letter for twenty years."
   - **Action**: he produces from his inside drawer a single yellowed envelope and sets it on the blotter, not opened.
   - **Expectation**: she has no expectation; he has destroyed hers.
   - **Reaction**: she does not move.
   - **Gap**: wide. The Counter-Idea is in the room, embodied; he is *not* asking her to be silent — he is offering her the same refusal her father offered him. *He is asking her to refuse the debt by doing her work*, and his work is the racket.
   - **(This is the beat where the audience must read Wei as morally serious. He is not pleading. He is showing her the only language in which his life makes sense.)**

5. **TURNING POINT / lie-exposed (the lie being her own).** She has come to extract acknowledgement so she can file truthfully. She now sees that he has *acknowledged* — not the racket, but its frame. He has named, by way of her father's letter, the price she came to pay, and the price he is paying, and the impossibility of the third path. He picks up the endorsement letter.
   - **Action**: he sets down his pen on the signed page, square at the corner, without looking at her.
   - **Expectation**: she does not yet know what to expect; her structure of expectation has collapsed.
   - **Reaction**: he says, "Take it to the registry before five. The bureau wants it stamped today." His hand moves the letter across the blotter to her.
   - **Gap**: at maximum width — between the *kindness* of his act (signing her endorsement; not asking her for anything) and the *consequence* of accepting it (she leaves carrying his protection now committed to paper, and her moral high ground is gone). *This is the flip.*

6. **Aftershock / withdraw — pulled-back.** She rises, takes the letter. She is two steps to the door when he speaks again.
   - **Action**: she turns her father's wristwatch a notch tighter against her wrist.
   - **Wei's line**: "敏敏. Whatever you decide, I will sign it." This is the only moment in the scene where he uses her childhood name. It is also the only moment where he refers, however obliquely, to her decision; and he refers to it not as a moral question but as a procedural one. He has placed the choice on her without naming it.
   - **Expectation**: she will say something — *anything*.
   - **Reaction**: she says, "嗯. Thank you, Supervisor." And leaves.
   - **Gap**: small (the formal closing); but the value charge has already flipped. The cool-down beat exists to let the audience feel the flip settle.

## 5. Turning Point

> **Beat 5 — Wei sets down his pen on the signed endorsement letter, square at the corner, and slides the page across to her without looking up.** The signature is the visible event; the irreversibility is in his refusal to use the moment to ask her for anything. He has signed her future without a condition, which has the effect of placing every condition inside her.

## 6. Subtext

Yu Min came to extract *language* — denial, confession, plea. Wei refuses language and instead enacts *the position*: he tells her father's letter; he signs the endorsement; he uses her childhood name once. Under his words: he is showing her that the only way through this is the way her father chose — to refuse the debt by doing the work, and to leave the rest unsaid. Under his actions: he is letting her keep the moral high ground if she can; he is not asking. Under her words: she is grateful, formally, twice; she is rehearsing not to be. Under her silence at beat 3: she has discovered that she came not to file but to be told she could not file; and he is refusing to tell her either thing. The scene's actual transaction is the silent transfer of the choice from his desk to her bag, sealed in the endorsement letter.

## 7. Eight-Point Scene Audit
- [x] **Turns** — opening charge + → closing charge − on the named value (integrity / complicity).
- [x] **Earned through conflict** — the conflict is Wei's *refusal* to give Yu Min the dialogue she came for; that refusal is itself an action she must answer.
- [x] **Turning Point is locatable** — Beat 5, the pen set down square on the signed page.
- [x] **Scene objective is playable** — "to extract acknowledgement without naming the racket" is a thing an actor can do.
- [x] **Gap is present** — every beat has a measurable gap; beats 3, 4, and 5 are *wide*.
- [x] **Minimum, conservative action honored** — Yu Min does not blow up the scene with a direct accusation, which would be premature and uncharacteristic; Wei does not deny or plead, the *smallest* available move; the file stays in her bag; nothing is destroyed in this scene; the costs are entirely interior. Both characters take the smallest play that might work.
- [x] **Subtext is alive** — text and subtext diverge throughout; the dialogue carries 25% of the scene's meaning, the actions carry the rest. Silent-screenplay test: passes for beats 1, 2, 5, 6; partial for 3, 4 (the line about her father's letter is doing real work).
- [x] **Serves the spine** — this is PC3 / Obligatory Scene 2 / the Mid-Act 2 turn. It closes off the escape "I can confront him directly and extract acknowledgement"; from this scene forward Yu Min knows the third path does not exist.
