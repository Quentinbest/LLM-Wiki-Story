# Antagonism Test — 错号 / Wrong Account

Audit-draft mode: scoring *delivered* antagonism on the page against the *promised* antagonism in the spine, with special scrutiny of the deliberate no-physical-threat design. (Ch.14 [[principle-of-antagonism]], [[negation-of-the-negation]], [[levels-of-conflict]].)

## 0. The design wager — stated plainly

This story stakes everything on a counterintuitive McKee claim: forces of antagonism need not be a villain (Ch.14). Here the antagonism is **gentleness, procedure, and attention-elsewhere**. The question is not "is it violent enough" but "is the *erasure* heavy enough that 陈默's victory costs him everything." The wager mostly pays off. The prose has found a genuinely rare antagonism register — being *listened to but never named*, being *seen but used*, being *helped without being recognized*. That is real Negation-of-the-Negation material. The failures are local, not structural.

## 5. The Crisis dilemma test

> Two irreconcilable goods, not lesser evils. (Ch.13 [[dilemma]].)

| Option A | Option B | Truly irreconcilable? | What forces the fork? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accept 墨渊: walk out with Su Han, gain the only being-seen ever offered him, ratify that 陈默 was never real | Refuse both names: speak "我是陈默，外卖员，工号 887423" into the intercom the System must register; recover legal identity but accept the room will empty and the faces that knew his face will forget within a week | **Yes.** Both are genuine goods (being-seen vs. being-true); each annihilates the other; neither is the obviously-correct moral choice once PC-5 has made being-seen-as-魔尊 *feel* good | The smoke (extra-personal) + 李奶奶's wound (personal) + the two live phones (both factions present) + his own residual dignity (inner) collapse into one stairwell |

**Verdict: this is a true dilemma, and the prose earns it.** The single highest-risk predicate the spine flagged — that the reader must have *felt the pull* of being-seen-as-魔尊 — is delivered in 3.3 (the dossier of his own kindnesses, his real cat-rescue and door-knocking returned to him as evidence he existed). Because the reader has felt that pull, Option A in the Crisis is not a strawman. Without 3.3 the dilemma would be fake; with it, it holds.

## 6. Counter-Idea embodiment check

> **Counter-Idea**: 真实身份先于世界的命名而存在——你是谁，与别人是否认识你无关。 (True identity precedes the world's naming; who you are is independent of whether others know you.)

| Carrier | Best scene | Could the audience plausibly agree with them there? |
|---|---|---|
| Lin Sheng | 2.4 (the "A+", the晕针 confirmation, the listening), 3.4–3.5 (the breakfast, "我说服的高层") | **Yes — dangerously so.** In 2.4 Lin Sheng is the first person in years to write down what 陈默 says; the reader *wants* this to be recognition. The Counter-Idea is at its most persuasive precisely because Lin Sheng is sincere and kind. This is the embodiment working at full strength. |
| Su Han | 3.3 (the roof, the dossier, the load-bearing line) | **Yes.** Her accuracy ("she sees him most precisely yet still calls him 魔尊") makes the Counter-Idea's failure *feel* like its triumph. The scene executes the character file's seven-point delivery spec faithfully (降调, the two-second silence, looking away after, not pushing). |

The Counter-Idea has not one but two persuasive embodiments, and the story defeats it *dramatically* (3.5: even the kind version never asks his name; 4.4: the correct name arrives with an apology and a departure) rather than by argument. This is the audit's second strongest finding. No melodrama; no cardboard antagonist. Rule against flimsy Counter-Idea: **passed decisively.**

## 7. Eight-Point Antagonism Audit
- [x] All three levels engaged across spine — Crisis & Climax both fire three ×s; only PC-1 is single-level (acceptable).
- [x] Negative Spectrum reaches Negation of the Negation — delivered on the page (3.3, 4.6), not merely labeled.
- [~] Antagonism escalates — mostly monotonic; **PC-2 is a local dip** (the only beat that softens below its neighbors). Not a sag across Act 2 as a whole; a single soft scene.
- [x] Antagonism out-resources protagonist for the whole story — no Pos > Neg window.
- [x] Risk real at every act end — Act I (frozen account), Act II (the path opens / he stays), Act III (bystander wounded, he cannot stay between names), Act IV (the dilemma).
- [x] Crisis is a true dilemma — two genuine goods; earned by 3.3.
- [x] Inner antagonism dramatized, not announced — the 30-extra-seconds motif (staying at the noodle table 2.4; not refusing on the roof 3.3; the hand at the pocket edge 2.7 and 4.2) is pure behavior. No confided monologue anywhere. Exemplary.
- [x] Counter-Idea has persuasive embodiment — two of them.

Single soft item: escalation, due solely to PC-2.

## 8. Does the middle (Act 2) sag?

**No — but it plateaus once.** Act 2 is the riskiest zone for under-opposition in any story, and here the design (gentle antagonism) magnifies the risk. The verdict:

- PC-3 (2.3–2.5) is the *strongest* antagonism in Act 2 and possibly the book: the 47-minute interrogation where being-listened-to is the trap, and 陈默 answers two extra questions because he doesn't want to leave (2.4: "因为没人替我记，记错了扣的是我的钱" — the involuntary self-revelation). The middle's engine is intact and turning.
- PC-4 (2.6–2.7) escalates correctly — a second faction, a path *downward*, the trembling spoon, the hand at the pocket.
- **The dip is PC-2 (2.1–2.2)**, the sister scene, which sits early in Act 2 and reads gentler than the spine intends — distraction rather than active erasure. Because PC-3 immediately re-escalates, the middle does not collapse; it has one soft step on an otherwise rising staircase.

So: the middle does not sag structurally. It has one under-opposed scene (2.2) that should be tightened so its gentle-erasure is *active* (a near-recognition that fails) rather than *ambient* (a busy sister). Fixing 2.2 makes Act 2 monotonic.

## 9. Verdict

**YELLOW** — The antagonism is structurally sound and, at its best (PC-3, PC-5/3.3, the Counter-Idea embodiment, the Crisis dilemma, the Resolution), genuinely sublime in the McKee sense: it reaches the Negation of the Negation and dramatizes it rather than labeling it. The no-physical-threat wager is load-bearing, not decorative — gentleness, procedure, and attention-elsewhere do the work that violence would do in a lesser draft. Three local weak points hold it back from green: (1) PC-2/sister (2.2) under-delivers its gentle-erasure and creates the only escalation dip; (2) PC-7's physical fight risks reading as low-stakes since 陈默's body is never threatened — protect the displacement onto 李奶奶 and onto the useless force-field; (3) the Climax force-balance is slack (the System grants the correct name almost frictionlessly) — meaning-stakes carry it, but a half-beat of System resistance would make the victory fought-for. None require structural revision. Targeted scene-level deepening on 2.2 and an optional half-beat at 4.3 move this to green.

## 10. Open questions for the writer
- 2.2: is the sister's gentleness meant to be *distraction* or *failure-to-see*? The spine wants the latter; the prose currently delivers the former. Confirm which.
- 4.3: do you want the System's correction to be frictionless (free grace, ironic) or fought-for (a flicker of resistance)? Either is defensible; pick consciously.
- PC-7: is the genre-obligatory sect fight pulling its weight, or is it tolerated as a contract obligation? The wounding works; the swordplay around it may be expendable.
