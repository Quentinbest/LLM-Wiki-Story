# Trading Places (1983)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/trading-places|中文]]

## Overview
A street hustler and a privileged broker are swapped in a social experiment staged by two wealthy brothers.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses Billy Ray Valentine's first reversal as a clean example of scene design: a desire in motion meets a world response far larger than expected, and the scene turns his life in a new direction.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[turning-point]]** — Billy Ray's fortunes flip from beggar to broker.
- **[[scene-objective]]** — His immediate objective is survival, but the world's reaction transforms the scene.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The police interruption and Duke-brothers intervention demonstrate comic surprise that still obeys structural rigor.
