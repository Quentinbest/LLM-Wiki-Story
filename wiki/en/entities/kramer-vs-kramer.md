# Kramer vs. Kramer (1979)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/kramer-vs-kramer|中文]]

## Overview
A Manhattan advertising executive's wife leaves him with their young son; as he learns to father, she returns seeking custody. Based on Avery Corman's novel.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses the film as a paradigm of a clean [[inciting-incident]] (Joanna walks out), an unambiguous [[object-of-desire]] (keep his son), and a complete [[character-arc]] (absent father → real father). It exemplifies how a domestic [[spine]] can carry the full architecture of Archplot.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[inciting-incident]]** — Joanna's departure radically upsets Ted's life balance.
- **[[object-of-desire]]** — Custody of Billy.
- **[[spine]]** — Ted's pursuit of fatherhood.
- **[[character-arc]]** — From self-involved professional to present parent.
- **[[progressive-complications]]** — Job loss, courtroom setbacks, Joanna's return.
