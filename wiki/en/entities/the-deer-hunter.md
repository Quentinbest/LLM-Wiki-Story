# The Deer Hunter (1978)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/the-deer-hunter|中文]]

## Overview
A working-class hunting circle is shattered by Vietnam, and ordinary rituals gather tragic, archetypal weight.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses the film to show how symbolic charge can rise scene by scene until the final movement feels mythic rather than merely realistic. He also points to the mountain confrontation as a crisis that reveals the protagonist's deepest moral turn.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[symbolic-ascension]]** — Hunting imagery rises toward ritual, sacrifice, and mercy.
- **[[key-image]]** — The elevated visual logic condenses the story's meaning.
- **[[crisis]]** — Michael's decision not to kill is a profound inner reversal.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The mountaintop hunting scene is McKee's signature example of climactic moral stillness.
