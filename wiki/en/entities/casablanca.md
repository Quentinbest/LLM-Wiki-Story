# Casablanca (1942)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/casablanca|中文]]

## Overview
In wartime Morocco, a cynical American nightclub owner must choose between his rekindled love and aiding her husband, a Resistance leader.

## McKee's Usage
McKee cites *Casablanca* for its masterful [[subplot]] weaving: the Laszlo/letters-of-transit subplot complicates Rick's central desire, while the Strasser/Renault subplot builds the political antagonism. The airport farewell is the paradigmatic delivered [[obligatory-scene]].

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[subplot]]** — Laszlo and the Resistance line as complication of Rick's romance.
- **[[obligatory-scene]]** — The airport goodbye.
- **[[controlling-idea]]** — Love triumphs when surrendered for a greater cause.
- **[[character-arc]]** — Rick from disengaged cynic to committed idealist.
