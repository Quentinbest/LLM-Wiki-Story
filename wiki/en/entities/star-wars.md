# Star Wars (1977)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/star-wars|中文]]

## Overview
A farm boy joins a rebellion against an empire and must trust a larger calling to destroy the Death Star.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses the Death Star run as a model of crisis feeding directly into climax. Luke's final choice between technological certainty and intuitive trust gives the action its meaning.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[crisis]]** — Luke must choose how to make the decisive attempt.
- **[[obligatory-scene]]** — The promised confrontation with the empire's concentrated power.
- **[[story-climax]]** — The destruction of the Death Star is the irreversible payoff of the story's central line.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The trench run shows how a brief crisis can instantly explode into a climactic event.
