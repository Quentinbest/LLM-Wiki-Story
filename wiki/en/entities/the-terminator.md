# The Terminator (1984)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/the-terminator|中文]]

## Overview
An ordinary waitress becomes the target of a machine sent from the future and, in surviving it, moves toward myth.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses the film to show symbolic progression through setting and action. Los Angeles gradually becomes a labyrinth, and Sarah Connor rises from ordinary worker to a figure of archetypal maternity and survival.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[symbolic-ascension]]** — Urban geography transforms into mythic structure.
- **[[key-image]]** — Sarah's final framing seals the story's archetypal lift.
- **[[story-climax]]** — The final defeat of the machine irreversibly changes Sarah's role in the world.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The descent into the maze-like industrial space completes the story's symbolic rise.
