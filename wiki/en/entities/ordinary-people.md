# Ordinary People (1980)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/ordinary-people|中文]]

## Overview
A suburban family fractures after the accidental death of their eldest son; the surviving younger son struggles with guilt while the mother refuses to grieve.

## McKee's Usage
A key example of [[backstory]] driving a present-day drama: the drowning of Buck and Beth's withholding shape every current scene. Also a paradigm of [[cast-design]] — four family members who respond differently to the same event.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[backstory]]** — Buck's drowning, Beth's reaction.
- **[[cast-design]]** — Polarized attitudes within one family.
- **[[character-arc]]** — Conrad's arc from guilt to self-acceptance.
- **[[levels-of-conflict]]** — Inner (Conrad's guilt), personal (parents' marriage), and social (community appearances) in every scene.
