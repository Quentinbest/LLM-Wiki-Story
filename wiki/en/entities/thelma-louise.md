# Thelma & Louise (1991)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/thelma-louise|中文]]

## Overview
Two friends on the run are cornered at the edge of the Grand Canyon and must choose their final freedom.

## McKee's Usage
McKee cites the ending as a model of the deliberately prolonged crisis. The repeated hesitation over the word "go" stores tension until the decision detonates into the climactic act.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[dilemma]]** — Prison or death; neither option preserves ordinary life.
- **[[crisis]]** — The final pause is the true dramatic center.
- **[[story-climax]]** — The leap turns the story irreversibly and completely.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The car at the canyon edge is McKee's prime example of crisis as compressed stillness.
