# Sullivan's Travels (1941)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/sullivan-travels|中文]]

## Overview
A successful comedy director decides to make a work of social significance and discovers, through humiliation, what his art is actually for.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses the film to illustrate layered setup and payoff. Sullivan's vanity, research quest, and prison experience all converge when he realizes that laughter is not trivial but necessary.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[setup-and-payoff]]** — The early satire of self-important art pays off in the prison theater revelation.
- **[[controlling-idea]]** — Entertainment becomes an ethical act when it answers suffering honestly.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The prison screening reinterprets everything that came before and redirects the film's meaning.
