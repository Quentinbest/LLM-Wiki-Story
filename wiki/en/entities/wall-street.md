# Wall Street (1987)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/wall-street|中文]]

## Overview
A hungry young broker enters the orbit of Gordon Gekko and trades integrity for access, wealth, and power.

## McKee's Usage
McKee uses Bud Fox's meeting with Gekko as a turning-point model. A business pitch becomes a moral test, and Bud's choice instantly turns both his fortune and his inner life.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[turning-point]]** — Gekko's reply cracks Bud's expectations.
- **[[scene-objective]]** — Bud enters to win approval and exits having redefined success itself.
- **[[character-arc]]** — The scene plants the arc from striver to corrupter.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The office encounter shows how one choice under pressure can reverse value on multiple levels at once.
