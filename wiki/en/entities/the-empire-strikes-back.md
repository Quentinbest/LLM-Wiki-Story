# The Empire Strikes Back (1980)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/entities/the-empire-strikes-back|中文]]

## Overview
Luke Skywalker confronts Darth Vader and discovers that the conflict is more intimate, morally unstable, and personally devastating than he knew.

## McKee's Usage
McKee repeatedly returns to this film because its climax keeps reopening the gap. Revelation, mutilation, temptation, and refusal each generate a fresh crisis and a deeper flood of insight.

## Concepts Illustrated
- **[[crisis]]** — Luke faces one impossible choice after another.
- **[[setup-and-payoff]]** — Earlier anxiety around Luke and Vader pays off on a much larger scale.
- **[[inevitable-and-unexpected]]** — The revelation shocks, yet retrospectively reorganizes the whole design.

## Key Scenes Analyzed
The Vader confrontation is McKee's go-to case of a climax that keeps turning.
