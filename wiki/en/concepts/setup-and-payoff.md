# Setup and Payoff

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/concepts/setup-and-payoff|中文]]

## Definition
**Setup and Payoff** is the pattern of planting knowledge early so that later turns, revelations, and climaxes feel inevitable when they arrive.

## McKee's Argument
McKee argues that insight is impossible without preparation. When a turning point hurls the audience back through the story asking "Why?", the answers must already be embedded in the work. Strong setups often carry one meaning at first and a deeper meaning later.

## How It Works
```mermaid
flowchart LR
  setup[Setup]
  fores[Foreshadowing]
  tp[Turning Point]
  payoff[Payoff]
  climax[[Story Climax]]
  setup --> fores --> tp --> payoff --> climax
```

Payoffs are rarely terminals. Once a payoff lands, it often becomes the next setup, creating a chain of revelation and redirected action.

## Film Examples
- **[[chinatown]]** — Noah Cross's line about "my daughter" flowers into multiple levels of meaning.
- **[[sullivan-travels]]** — Sullivan's social ambition is paid off and reinterpreted by prison laughter.
- **[[the-empire-strikes-back]]** — The paternal revelation pays off two films of planted anxiety.

## Relationship to Other Concepts
- [[foreshadowing]] — A common instrument for setting up later meaning.
- [[turning-point]] — The moment that activates the prepared material.
- [[story-climax]] — The largest payoff in the work.
- [[key-image]] — The final image often seals a long chain of preparation.

## Common Mistakes
Setups fail when they are either invisible or obvious. Underprepare the unusual and the payoff feels random; overprepare it and the audience arrives first.
