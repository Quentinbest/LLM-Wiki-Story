# Notes on Chapter 11: Scene Analysis

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/notes/chapter-11-notes|中文]]

## On Text and Subtext

> "Text refers to the sensory surface of a work of art. In film, that's the images on screen, along with the soundtrack of dialogue, music, and effects. It is what we see, what we hear, what characters say, what characters do. Subtext is the life beneath that surface — the known and unknown thoughts and feelings hidden by the behavior."

## Reflection

- Text is the surface; subtext is the essence beneath the surface. Don't stop at what you see — see *through* it.
- For a writer this becomes a working habit: never script only the surface. At every moment, ask what is *actually* happening underneath what appears to be happening.
