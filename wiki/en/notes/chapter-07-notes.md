# Notes on Chapter 7: The Substance of Story

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/notes/chapter-07-notes|中文]]

## On Sympathy vs. Empathy

- *Sympathetic* — feeling for someone.
- *Empathetic* — feeling as someone.

**The protagonist must be empathetic**, because empathy is the glue of the audience's emotional investment.

## McKee's Passage

> "When we identify with a protagonist and his desires in life, we are in fact rooting for our own desires in life. Through empathy — that feeling of being at one with a fictional person — we test and extend our own humanity. A story gives us the chance to live lives beyond our own, to lodge in other times and places, to quest, struggle, feel the various depths of our own existence."

## Reflection

> Empathy tests and extends our own humanity.

Empathy is not pity; it is *inhabiting*. The audience stretches the self by becoming, for two hours, the [[protagonist]]. Stories give us controlled practice at being other people without losing who we are.
