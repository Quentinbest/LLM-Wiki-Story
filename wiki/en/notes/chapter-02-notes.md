# Notes on Chapter 2: The Structure Spectrum

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/notes/chapter-02-notes|中文]]

## On Structure — What and Why

McKee's one-sentence definition captures both *what* structure is and *why* it exists:

> "STRUCTURE is a selection of events from the characters' life stories that is composed into a strategic sequence to arouse specific emotions and to express a specific view of life."

The two purposes of composing events: to express one's emotions and thoughts, and to express a worldview to the world. Story art is one way of expressing values to the world.

## On Story Events as Functions

The full definition: "A Story Event creates meaningful change in the life situation of a character that is expressed and experienced in terms of a value and ACHIEVED THROUGH CONFLICT."

**A computational analogy:** An event can be viewed as a function:
- **Input:** The starting value state (e.g., justice)
- **Output:** The ending value state (e.g., injustice)
- **Processing:** The conflict that achieves the transformation from input to output

For example, an event that transforms someone from justice to injustice — the input and output are the value poles (justice/injustice), and the transformation between them is accomplished through conflict.

This is an elegant mental model: *event = change*, *value = the axis of change (positive/negative)*, *conflict = the mechanism of change*.
