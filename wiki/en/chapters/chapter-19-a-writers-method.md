# Chapter 19: A Writer's Method

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/chapters/chapter-19-a-writers-method|中文]]

## Summary
The difference between struggling writers and professionals is not talent; it is **method**. McKee contrasts two work patterns:

- **Outside in** — The struggling writer dreams an idea, rushes to the keyboard, writes description and dialogue until page 120, and then rewrites by *preserving favorite scenes* while pretzeling story around them. He ends in burnout with a tissue-thin draft.
- **Inside out** — The professional spends the first four of six months on **3x5 cards** building a [[step-outline]]. Ninety percent of what he invents is destroyed in patient pursuit of quality. Only after the outline pitches well to listeners does he write a full **[[treatment]]** — moment-by-moment descriptions of action and subtext, but *no dialogue* — often sixty to ninety pages. Only then does he convert treatment to screenplay, adding dialogue last. The first draft is a joy; five to ten pages a day.

**[[writing-from-the-inside-out|Writing from the inside out]]**, now applied at project scale, is the method that makes dialogue character-specific and scenes load-bearing. Premature dialogue chokes creativity: the writer falls in love with his lines, cannot play with events, and "rewrites" become tinkering.

## Key Concepts Introduced
- **[[step-outline]]** — The story on 3x5 cards: one or two sentences per scene, stack per act, purpose labeled on the back.
- **[[treatment]]** — Expansion of each scene into a paragraph of present-tense action + a full subtext of thought and feeling; 60-90 pages; no dialogue.
- **[[a-writers-method]]** — The full inside-out workflow: outline → pitch → treatment → screenplay → polishes.
- Deepened: **[[writing-from-the-inside-out]]** — Extends the scene-level principle to the whole project.

## Key Examples
- *Les Diaboliques* — McKee's sample pitch: if a ten-minute telling silences listeners, the step-outline is ready for treatment.
- Studio-era writers — Treatments of 200–300 pages meant no moment was unthought.

## McKee's Core Argument
"The premature writing of dialogue chokes creativity." Outside-in work is the slowest possible path because writers cannot abandon words they have typed; they wander for years before realizing the story was wrong. Inside-out work discovers this on the card table in weeks. It is both the most disciplined and the most free method because it postpones words until events, values, and subtext have been fully stress-tested.
