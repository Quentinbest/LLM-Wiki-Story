# Character Revelation

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/characters/character-revelation|中文]]

## Definition

The revelation of true character in contrast or contradiction to characterization is fundamental to all fine storytelling. Life teaches this grand principle: what *seems* is not what *is*. People are not what they appear to be. A hidden nature waits concealed behind a facade of traits.

## McKee's Argument

When characterization and true character match — when inner life and outer appearance are of one substance — the role becomes a list of repetitious, predictable behaviors. Such a character may be credible (shallow people exist), but they are boring.

Major characters must be written in depth — they cannot be at heart what they seem at face. Minor roles may or may not need hidden dimensions, but principals *must* have this gap between surface and depth.

## How It Works

- Establish a clear, specific characterization (the mask)
- Create pressure that forces choices revealing a deeper, contrasting or contradicting nature
- The gap between characterization and true character is where dramatic interest lives
- The audience's pleasure comes from the surprise of discovery: "So *that's* who they really are"

## Film Examples

- **James Bond** — Endless audience pleasure from the repeated revelation: lounge lizard on the surface, lethal genius beneath
- **Rambo in *First Blood*** — Vietnam burnout seeking solitude → ruthless, unstoppable killer. Compelling because of the contrast.
- **Rambo sequels** — Characterization merged with true character → boring, because there was no gap left to reveal

## Relationship to Other Concepts

- [[characterization-vs-true-character]] — Revelation is the dramatic moment that exposes the gap between these two
- [[character-arc]] — Revelation is the prerequisite; arc is what follows when the revealed character then changes

## Common Mistakes

- Creating protagonists who are exactly what they appear to be from first scene to last
- Confusing "surprising characterization" (unexpected quirks) with genuine character revelation
- Revealing true character too early, leaving nothing for later acts to expose
