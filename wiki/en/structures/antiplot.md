# Antiplot (Antistructure)

> 中文版：[[wiki/zh/structures/antiplot|中文]]

## Definition

Antiplot is the cinema counterpart to the antinovel and Theatre of the Absurd. It doesn't reduce the Classical but reverses it, contradicting traditional forms to exploit, perhaps ridicule, the very idea of formal principles. The Antiplot-maker is rarely interested in understatement; rather, films tend toward extravagance and self-conscious overstatement.

## Position in the Story Hierarchy

- **Position:** Lower-right corner of [[the-story-triangle]]
- **Contrast with:** [[archplot]] (which it reverses) and [[miniplot]] (which reduces classical form)
- **This level:** An antistructure variation that contradicts and subverts classical storytelling principles

## McKee's Argument

Antiplot exists to oppose classical form. It substitutes coincidence for causality, nonlinear time for chronology, and inconsistent realities for the stable fictional world. McKee notes that Antiplot works have always carried a vein of dark satire and that serious antistructure devices have largely become the staples of film farce. The audience for Antiplot is the smallest—loyal cinephile intellectuals who enjoy twisted realities.

## How It Works

Antiplot features (some or all):
- **Coincidence** — unmotivated events replace causal chains
- **Nonlinear time** — temporal order scrambled or impossible to sort
- **Inconsistent realities** — fictional rules broken or mixed arbitrarily
- The only rule is to break rules

## Film Examples

- *After Hours* — Coincidence-driven nightmare: a man bounces randomly through Soho like a pool ball on God's table
- *Bad Timing* — Time scrambled to express "character as destiny"
- *Weekend* (Godard) — Emily Brontë yanked from the 19th century onto a French road and set on fire; a rule made to be broken
- *Monty Python and the Holy Grail*, *Un Chien Andalou*, *Persona*, *Last Year at Marienbad*

## Relationship to Other Concepts

- [[archplot]] — Antiplot is a deliberate reversal of classical design
- [[miniplot]] — Both are non-classical; Miniplot reduces, Antiplot contradicts
- [[the-story-triangle]] — Occupies the lower-right position
- [[master-classical-form]] — McKee argues you must understand what you're reversing

## Common Mistakes

Creating antistructure without understanding classical form—rebellion without comprehension produces pretentiousness, not art. Also: believing that "difference for the sake of difference" is creative achievement.
