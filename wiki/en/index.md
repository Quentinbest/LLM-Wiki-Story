## Concepts
- [[structure]] — The internal system of events building to a climax expressing a specific meaning
- [[story-as-metaphor]] — Story as the essential equipment for living, a metaphor for life
- [[story-values]] — The universal qualities of human experience that shift through storytelling
- [[story-event]] — A meaningful change in a character's life situation expressed through value shifts
- [[story-form]] — The spectrum of narrative forms from pure event-driven to pure non-event cinema
- [[the-story-triangle]] — The three-cornered design map: Archplot, Miniplot, and Antiplot
- [[setting]] — The four-dimensional world of a story: Period, Duration, Location, Level of Conflict
- [[creative-limitation]] — The principle that constraints inspire rather than inhibit creativity
- [[research]] — Three methods of acquiring story knowledge: memory, imagination, and fact
- [[creative-choices]] — The discipline of overproduction and rigorous selection

## Structures
- [[beat]] — The smallest unit of story structure: an exchange of action/reaction
- [[scene]] — A unit of story built from beats, turning on a value shift through conflict
- [[sequence]] — A series of 2–5 scenes building to a moderate reversal
- [[act]] — A series of sequences climaxing in a major turning point
- [[story-climax]] — The final, irreversible change that answers the story's major dramatic question
- [[archplot]] — Classical Design: single protagonist, active, causally linked, closed ending
- [[miniplot]] — Minimalism: reduced narrative elements while retaining enough to engage
- [[antiplot]] — Anti-structure: deliberately reverses classical principles

## Principles
- [[no-scene-that-doesnt-turn]] — Every scene must contain a value change or it doesn't belong
- [[master-classical-form]] — Writers must master Archplot before experimenting with alternatives
- [[tell-story]] — McKee's imperative: the writer's first duty is to tell a story
- [[craft-maximizes-talent]] — Craft is the means to free creative talent, not restrict it
- [[war-on-cliche]] — The root of all cliché is ignorance of the story's world
- [[story-obeys-its-world]] — A story must obey its own internal laws of probability
- [[master-your-genre]] — Writers must master genre conventions to anticipate audience expectations
